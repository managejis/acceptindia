Umesh was very sick when he was brought to ACCEPT. As part of the legal procedures, we asked the family members whether they would like to have the dead body in case he died. And their reply was, “You do whatever you want”. This is the case of many who come to ACCEPT. In this place, many people found a home; they were accepted and treated well without any discrimination till their death.

ACCEPT was started as a small response to the plight of HIV/AIDS infected people. ACCEPT stands for AIDS Counselling, Care, Education, Prevention, and Training. ACCEPT Society strives to meet the medical, social, psychological, economic, and spiritual needs of the HIV infected in India. Based out of Bangalore, ACCEPT is today recognized by NACO (Govt. of India) as the only A - Grade Care Centre of its kind in Bangalore, where the prevalence of HIV infected is comparatively high. We started with 5 patients and now we cater to an average of 70 patients every month through our in-patient and out-patient facilities. Now, we have 35-bed capacity. The overall number of HIV+ people benefited from our service is more than 900 excluding the numerous family members who have benefited. We have seen as many as 180 deaths in our centre as many patients are brought in the last stage of disease.
