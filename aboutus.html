Introduction:

HIV/AIDS for the past two decades, has been reigning as the biggest challenge faced by mankind, killing more people than the World Wars and the light at the end of the tunnel, of slowing down the onslaughter of this killer disease, is still far away. Millions all over the world are writhing under the throes of HIV/AIDS, but more so the people in the SouthEast Asia Region. India, battling a losing war against HIV/AIDS, has the dubious distinction of being the current world leader in the number of HIV infected cases, with an estimated four to six million cases in a population of over a billion.

At point in history have we been more in need of the Gentle Healer than in the present. Misunderstood, unloved and rejected, HIV/AIDS patients fear each new daywith its brand new hurts and hopelessness. As of now death is the only cure, and the burden of unsaved lives makes the trasnsition into the land of the living a nightmare. HIV/AIDS is no longer the disease that happens to other people. Tomorrow it could catch up with you or your dear ones.

Living in cocooned world as we do, we often tend to view HIV/AIDS as God's punishment to people who brought down the disease upon themselves with their immoral lifestyles. When we look at the facts we will be shocked to find many infected with no fault of their own including women and children.

Our life, like every other blessing we receive, defines its worth from its use alone. No grace is truly ours till we ensure that God blesses someone else with it through us. Our time on earth is but a breath, and our responsibilities unlimited. We know a peace and hope that lives beyond the boundaries of this world. We will each be held accountable if we do not share this eternal message with a community that is voicelessly crying out for help.

History:

In 1981, doctors in the United States of America diagnosed four cases of a strange infection that baffled them. Similar cases were being diagnosed in other parts of the world, especially Africa. The infection lead to different kinds of complications like tuberculosis and pneumonia, during the course of which the patient lost his immunity and became susceptible to every infection, eventually leading to a slow and painful death. The disease was named AIDS- Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome. It took some time to discover the culprit, a tiny virus that was named HIV-Human Immunodeficiency Virus. By 1993, more people were dying of AIDS in the United States than the number of people killed during the Vietnam War. Sometimes cases were getting completely out of control in Africa, Thailand, Eastern Europe and Asia. Certain African countries like Botswana are facing extinction in the face of widespread AIDS. One in three people are living with HIV/AIDS. Life expectancy is expected to fall as low as 29 years. A certain newspaper report warns that 'South African population could be down by 71 per cent in 10 years due to AIDS.

Statistics:

According to reports , there are over 40 million affected or infected by HIV/AIDS worldwide. AIDS, according to the United Nations report in 1999, is the fourth largest killer after heart disease, stroke and respiratory infections. 21.8 million deaths have occurred due to AIDS since the start of the epidemic. In the year 2000 alone, 3 million people died of AIDS and 5.3 million were infected. South Africa and India are leading in the number of total infected people. Over 13 million children are estimated to have lost either one or both their parents to the disease.

Asia accounts for 25 per cent of all HIV affected cases in the world. With India leading the pack with four to six million cases reported and threatening to touch the ten million mark in the near future. Karnataka ranks second in the country after Maharastra in terms of HIV/AIDS infected or affected cases. Statistics indicate that there could be over 15 lakhs cases in Karnataka alone. A newspaper article states that the number of HIV patients in one of the hospitals in Bangalore has doubled in a year.

This killer disease is posing a great threat not only to the so called 'modern hi-tech' cities but has spread its tentacles to the smaller urban areas as well as rural India. Research is still on to find a way to combat this little virus causing such unprecedented and widespread destruction, not only to those infected but also to those affected by it. Till then, the world must bear silent witness to the death of countless people, majority of them in the prime of their life.

Everyone's problem:

India being a developing country, is reeling under the magnitude of devastation caused by HIV/AIDS. In a land of over a billion people, with few millions infected, with hundreds of new cases being reported every day, one of the magazine has described the Indian situation as a time bomb ready to explode. Termed a global disaster and the Third World War in disguise, HIV/AIDS has managed to cause a deep personal suffering and disgrace to those infected, like no other. And for a country whose economy is already cracking under pressure, HIV/AIDS is an added unbearable burden as well as loss, of productive manpower, since the infection mostly affects the productive segment of the society, those in the age group of 18 to 45, a national calamity indeed!

Many of those affected with HIV/AIDS come from very poor families in the rural areas. The mean age of the patients is 25-30 years and by profession, they are mostly businessmen, servicemen, farmers and truck drivers. Many of them are sole bread-winner of the family, which means that the whole family is severely affected by the pandemic. The women, as in the case always, is affected most. Many of them are the innocent victims in this, have lost their husband to the disease, are themselves HIV positive, and do not have an income or support from the society.

There is no proven cure for HIV/AIDS and anti-retroviral drugs and related tests that need to be carried out periodically (though cost of these medicines has come down considerably in the last year) makes treatment too expensive and out of the reach of 90 per cent of the Indian population. Severe adverse reaction to these drugs is also stated.

The problem facing our country is, therefore, far beyond the scope of the Government. The need for voluntary organizations to share this burden of caring for those affected and formulating prevention strategies is vital and has proved to be successful in many of the African countries in rehabilitating those in need and also in spreading awareness about AIDS. Voluntary organizations share a close interpersonal relationship with the people in the community they work in, making it extremely useful for implementing behavioural changes necessary for HIV/AIDS prevention and care. Voluntary organizations has greater flexibility than Government agencies to implement changes and can make quicker decisions with the support of the community.

In spite of few organizations in the field, aiming to spread awareness, care for those infected and rehabilitate those in need, the need is so great that the work will never get duplicated; there is always room for more.

Origin of ACCEPT:

Four years ago, Raju K. Mathew, concerned about the plight of AIDS victims, started visiting a nearby HIV/AIDS Care Centre, and was deeply moved by the impact of this epidemic on families, women and especially children. With a genuine concern to help and gain more knowledge on the epidemic and its prevention and care, Raju K. Mathew attended workshops on HIV/AIDS and visited many care centers in India. The initial concern and vision was shared with other like minded people and after much prayer and consultation, seven of them joined together to form a charitable organization called ACCEPT-AIDS Counseling, Care, Education, Prevention Training -Society. The names uniqueness is understood when we realize that many of those affected with HIV/AIDS are rejected by homes and society due to the social stigma attached to it and unfounded fear and ignorance.

Taking into consideration the magnitude of the problem, ACCEPT aims to work in tandem with the other organizations involved in alleviating the suffering of those affected. In this regard we highly esteem those organizations and individuals who are already working hard in this field. ACCEPT is convinced that though there are many awareness programmes regarding this disease, caring for the people affected is still a needy area. What patients need is support, not sympathy. People go far looking for a place that will accept them and take care of them till their death or till they and their families learn to accept and look after themselves. ACCEPT wants to step into the gap and involve itself in caring for these affected ones, be it the patient or the family.


Aims and Objectives of ACCEPT:

  • To give holistic care to the HIV/AIDS affected patients and families with necessary spiritual, physical, emotional and psychological concern.
  • To provide medical and psychological relief through care centers
  • To conduct awareness programmes to prevent and control the spread of HIV/AIDS in India
  • To network with voluntary organizations working in the same field
  • To establish, expand and maintain care centers in different needy places in India
  • Help people identify and get out from destructive anti-family and social relationships that spread this disease and replace them with moral value based relationships
  • To work with people who are involved in high-risk professions and provide character building and alternate means of employment
  • To provide love, care and education to children orphaned or affected by HIV/AIDS
  • To train affected people and their dependents in skills and trades for self support to meet their basic needs and help them start income generating projects
  • To provide employment opportunities to the poor and needy irrespective of whether they are affected by HIV or not
  • To provide necessary spiritual and moral upliftment to those suffering from HIV/AIDS and to guide them into the path of eternal hope
  • To bring several health awareness and education in society to eliminate ignorance regarding HIV/AIDS transmittance and to encourage families and socities to accept and respect people suffering with HIV/AIDS
  • To equip the patient to face death with dignity, when it becomes inevitable
Progress so far:

ACCEPT has been involved , for the past few years in visiting care centers, counseling people, attending seminars and workshops on AIDS and studying the working of a hospice, in order to start our own care center.
We have a small help column in all the 13 newspapers in the city and many contact us through our help line and we follow up with counseling, taking the patient to the doctor and on going visits to help them cope with the fear and other problems. We could see real changes in the lives of many due to the grace of God through our counseling. Many who were thinking of putting the infected person in some care center are now able to care for them at home and we are so thrilled to see this acceptance at their own home.

Through letters and personal visits to concerned people we were able to raise enough funds to buy a land of 1 Ľ acre and start building a 20 bed, 14 room hospice. The building is about 70 % complete with flooring, electrical, plumbing, painting and furnishing need yet to be taken care off. There is also need to install a solar water heating system .We are praying and hoping to open the center to people in need by first week of 2002.

ACCEPT is a member of CANA, a national alliance of voluntary organizations in the field of HIV/AIDS care.
