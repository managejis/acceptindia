Project Compassion Care Centre:

ACCEPT is building a hospice/care centre near Visthar and KRC at Doddagubbi, Bangalore rural. The aim of the center is to care for the destitute with HIV/AIDS and rehabilitate them into mainstream society as much as possible.

Aims:
To provide a counseling education and care center for about 20 desperately needy affected ones and many others on an outpatient basis.

- To provide immediate medical and other support to meet their needs
- To give unconditional love and acceptance
- To provide income generating support to patients or their dependants.
- To be a resource center for awareness creation and care for people affected and their relatives

Activities:

An outpatient clinic serves to screen patients coming in and to provide appropriate help. T he eligibility of the patient for the care center will be determined at the clinic. Those patients who come from very poor families and who are proving to be a burden to their families qualify to be admitted in the center. The aim is to equip them with skills and knowledge so that they can become self-reliant and can stand on their own feet as long as possible.

A laboratory for conducting tests required to determine the presence of HIV at a rational cost, open to anyone who want to conduct the test.

Education and awareness building programmes in the community, aimed at educating the society on the ways in which HIV spreads and how it can be prevented. There could be workshops, seminars, house-to-house programmes, where ACCEPT volunteers would take classes for the family members, teach basic protection methods in caring for the sick at home and suggesting other ways of income generation.

Responsibility and value building projects. The community will be made aware of its responsibility towards those affected by HIV/AIDS and educated on how to take care of those persons. There will be classes on spiritual and moral values and character building.

Beneficiaries:

Phase I of the project provides in-house care for about 20 people and counseling and outpatient care to hundreds including the family members of those in the care center.

Phase II, III, IV of the project will include hospice to care for more than 50 people who are destitute. Awareness creation, education and outpatient department would cater, not only to those infected but also to their family members, with special emphasis on children and women.

Justification:

Bangalore city and its rural areas have countless thousands who are affected directly or indirectly by this dreaded disease and are in need of care and acceptance. This includes many who move in here from other parts of the State. The need is so great that there will be enough work for many more voluntary organizations in this field. The need of the hour is professional, committed organizations networking with each other to alleviate this phenomenal problem. There are affected people desperately looking for help. Personal visits to HIV/AIDS care centers over the last 4 years, talking to many persons who are affected, the request of many who are looking for acceptance and support, the magnitude of the problem facing our nation and the availability and willingness of ACCEPT and the joy comes from serving people in need are the justification and rationale of this project. There are doctors from few hospitals who has promised to work with us and our counselors are ready to work with people in their home setting as we believe that home care is the best care.
