Our Staff Team

 

The centre is run by a team that includes Administrative staff, Doctor, Nursing/Lab Staff, and Counsellor. Team members are conscious of the special needs and problems of HIV positive and AIDS patients and handle situations with compassion, tact, dedication and patience, looking upon their work not merely as a job but as a mission of service.

Meet Our Dedicated Staff

Dr. Timothy Philip

At ACCEPT I treat the HIV and AIDS cases providing them with proper medication. Have been working here for past one and half years. Nutrition and proper palliative cure is required for the patients because the virus cannot be killed.
God has given me a chance to serve the under-privileged. When patients get proper treatment and show recovery it gives me great joy. I have a positive attitude.

Joseph Ulahanah

I am the administrator in ACCEPT and was recently appointed to this post. My role is to streamline and make the work process transparent. In my administration patient care is the most important focus. Staff should orient and dedicate themselves towards the patients. Here I should give credit to the staff because of their commitment towards the Lord. ACCEPT staff is cohesive and the best I have worked with in my career. They are so selfless.
I need to be a missionary in my life. This motivates me to work in ACCEPT.

Nataraj

My job is to counsel the patients during their admission to ACCEPT or while they stay here. I give them basic info on HIV and AIDS. And also inform them of the rules and regulations in ACCEPT. Counseling includes pre-test and post-test. There is also a regular class conducted for the inmates helping them understand discrimination and stigma on HIV patients and how they can cope with it. Here we prepare them mentally, emotionally and physically. Education and workshop on hygiene, nutrition and medicine are conducted.
Special instructions and care is given to TB patients. They are taught on sputum care and hygiene. In case the family rejects the patients we call them and give them hope. Beyond this we have seen success when families accept the patient back to them. Orphan patients are referred to other NGO’s for care and shelter. Currently six patients have got jobs in ACCEPT itself.
Daily the love of Christ motivates me to come and work in ACCEPT. The Lord Jesus did so many miracles on this earth to comfort the helpless. When He cured the leper He touched him. I am touched by the great compassion of our Creator and Lord, the great shepherd of our souls.
I hear about the rejections each patient faces. Everyday is a learning experience while I serve patients groaning with pain and sufferings.

Vinoy KA

Past three I have been working in agriculture, diary, irrigation and maintenance. I want to serve the Lord and ACCEPT has given me that opportunity. God gives me strength to continue in my service. Here I love to do things with joy.
In my youth I was driven into many bad things. I am so glad that one day I heard the gospel and got saved. After me my two brothers and two sisters also got saved. I have six brothers and four sisters. Praying for salvation of all of them.
Now there is a road that would be going through ACCEPT compound. That would sure redesign my activity.

Anila Vinoy

I am a staff nurse in ACCEPT for the past three years and would like to happily continue to care for the patients that come here.
I hail from a RC family where my father was a drunkard. We are five sisters and one brother. My sisters were pious and very active in choir and Church activities. Our neighbor was a believer and we were touched by their lives. We attended Sunday School at their house. We loved to hear them sing. One day a gospel meeting was conducted and my father got saved. But we suffered great persecution from our Church. By God’s grace my two sisters got married to Lord’s servants. I myself am married to a man serving God with me. One of my sisters, Sunita is also working with me in ACCEPT with her husband who is a driver.
Our morning duty includes giving food and water to helpless patients who can’t even lift their hands. Also they do not have their friends nor relatives to care for. It give me immense joy to be to them ‘a friend in need’. We have two daughters Kazia (3.5) and Nissia (1.5).

Blesson K Philip

At ACCEPT I am an accountant and a health worker. I also take care of the stores. There is great joy in working for the poor. I like ACCEPT. Past two years at work I have found that at ACCEPT we all work together. We see death and shaken lives. At the same time I am thrilled by the involvement of a loving and caring staff. I am thankful to God for giving me this opportunity to work here. Though there are difficulties, which surpass my understanding God has given me power in Christ.
In past I did not know God neither His ways. I got saved in 1997 and since then the Lord has corrected my worldly ways. He proved Himself faithful when I faced difficulties in education and personal life. He also guided my footsteps to ACCEPT and I am very happy with this. It is my first job experience.
