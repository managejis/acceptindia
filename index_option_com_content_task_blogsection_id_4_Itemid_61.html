Counseling Services

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

ACCEPT is engaged in offering pre and post counselling for HIV patients and their families.

Telephonic counselling is also offered through a helpline advertised in local newspapers.

Health and hygenie counselling is also given to family members to take care of the HIV patients.

 
Care of HIV Orphan Children

THE PLIGHT OF HIV ORPHANS

 

The AIDS orphans are catergorised as affected and infected. 

India has 2.5 lakh infected children.

Over 2 million affected children live in community homes run by NGOs or railway platforms or streets.

Half a million affected children die under the age of 15 every year. They contact HIV through mother-to-child transmission.


Nine-year-old Dhanya was found along with her thirteen-year-old sister by a social worker. Both were under the influence of whitener sniffing and living on the platform of Bangalore Railway Stations for two years undergoing every kind of abuse. The social worker also discovered that Dhanya was HIV+ and brought her to ACCEPT. Here she found care and in six months she gained 5 kgs. Although she improved in health and physical condition her behaviour remained the same.

Regular counseling, guidance, emotional support, play therapy, Sunday school, children’s camp and visit to local church meetings has brought gradual change in Dhanya. A hyper active child with improper speech and habits has now given up bad words and goes to school regularly.

ACCEPT has a separate Children Home placed little away from the hospital. Two staffs are assigned to look after 15 children, presently. They also go to a local school at Kothanoor. We plan to open a school at ACCEPT.

 
Rehabilitation

We are also engaged in rehabilitating some of the patients who are healthy by offering them jobs in ACCEPT.  As well as we provide them jobs by networking with other NGOs.

We also want to conduct job training to some of the widows of deceased patients.

HIV/TB Care

 

HIV and TB go together. As HIV patient's immunity is weak the chance to get TB is more compared to others. Due to its infectious nature, many NGO's working with HIV patients care don't take HIV patients with TB.

This challenged us and we started a separate ward with 10 bed for such patients. ACCEPT also provides the DOT treatment for such patients.
