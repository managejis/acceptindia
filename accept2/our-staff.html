Home Our Staff

The Care Centre is run by a team that includes administrative staff, a doctor, Nursing/Lab staff, and a counsellor. Team members are conscious of the special needs and problems of HIV positive and AIDS patients and handle situations with compassion, tact, dedication, and patience. They look upon their work not merely as a job but as a mission of service.

Dr. Timothy Philip

At ACCEPT, I treat the HIV and AIDS cases providing them with proper medication. I have been working here for past 1-1/2 years. Nutrition and proper palliative cure is required for the patients because the virus cannot be killed. It gives me great joy when patients get proper treatment and show recovery. I thank God for this opportunity that he has given me to serve the under-privileged.

Joseph Ulahanah

I work as the administrator in ACCEPT. My role is to streamline and make the work process transparent. Patient care, of course, is our most important focus. It is extremely important for us that the staff orient and dedicate themselves toward the patients. Here, I should give credit to the staff because of their commitment towards the Lord. ACCEPT staff is most cohesive and the best I have worked with in my career. Their dedication and selflessness is the single most motivating factor at my work here.

Nataraj

My job is to counsel the patients during their admission to ACCEPT or while they stay here. I help them sort through the myths and understand the facts of HIV and AIDS. I also help them understand the rules and regulations in ACCEPT. Counselling includes a pre-test and post-test. There is also a regular class conducted for the patients who reside here, helping them understand discrimination and myths on HIV patients and how they can cope with it. Here, we prepare them mentally, emotionally, and physically. Education through workshops on hygiene, nutrition, and medicine are conducted. Special instructions and care is also given to TB patients. They are taught on sputum care and hygiene. In case the family rejects the patients, we call them and talk to them. In many cases, we have seen success when families accept the patient back to them. Orphan patients are referred to other NGOs for care and shelter. Currently 6 patients work in ACCEPT as helpers.

Daily the love of Christ motivates me to come and work in ACCEPT. The Lord Jesus did so many miracles on this earth to comfort the helpless. When He cured the leper, He touched him. I am touched by the great compassion of our Creator and Lord, the great Shepherd of our souls. I hear about the rejections each patient faces, but also the acceptance that they find in the Lord. Every day is a learning experience while I serve patients groaning with pain and suffering.

Vinoy KA

For the past 3 years I have been working in agriculture, diary, irrigation, and maintenance. I want to serve the Lord and ACCEPT has given me that opportunity. God gives me strength to continue in my service. Here I love to do things with joy. In my youth I was driven into many bad things. I am so glad that one day I heard the gospel and got saved. After me, my 2 brothers and 2 sisters also got saved. I have 6 brothers and 4 sisters and I continue to fervently pray for the salvation of all of them.

Anila Vinoy

I have been working as a staff nurse in ACCEPT for the past 3 years and would like to happily continue to care for the patients here. I hail from a Roman Catholic family where my father was a drunkard. Our family consists of 5 sisters and 1 brother. My sisters were pious and very active in choir and church activities. Our neighbour was a believer and we were touched by their lives. We attended Sunday School at their house. We loved to hear them sing. One day a gospel meeting was conducted and my father got saved. Though we suffered great persecution from our church, by God’s grace, my 2 sisters got married to the Lord’s servants. I myself am married to a man serving God with me. One of my sisters, Sunita is also working with me in ACCEPT with her husband who is a driver. Our morning duty includes giving food and water to helpless patients who can’t even lift their hands. As they do not have friends or relatives to care for them, it give me immense joy to be to them ‘a friend in need.’

Blesson K Philip

At ACCEPT I work as an accountant and a health worker. I also take care of the stores. There is great joy in working in ACCEPT. For the past 2 years at work I have found that at ACCEPT we get bonded to one another seeing the death and shaken lives. At the same time, I am thrilled by the love and caring my fellow colleagues show to the patients. I am thankful to God for giving me this opportunity to work here. He also guided my footsteps to ACCEPT and I am very happy with the work I do here.
